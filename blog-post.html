How to calculate the real CAC ceiling for your brand

Your margins and payback period set a hard limit on what you can pay to acquire a customer. Here is how to find that number and run against it.

Most founders set acquisition budgets by feel. They watch the blended number, they react when it climbs, and they hope the math works out. There is a cleaner way to run this, and it starts with one number: the most you can afford to pay for a customer and still grow profitably.

Start with contribution margin

Contribution margin is what is left from an order after the variable costs of fulfilling it: cost of goods, shipping, payment fees, and returns. It is the real money an order generates before you spend anything to acquire the customer.

Work it out per order, not per unit. Bundles, sample sizes, and free shipping thresholds all change the picture, so use your actual average order.

Decide how fast you need to get paid back

Your ceiling depends on how long you are willing to wait to recover acquisition cost. A brand funding growth from cash flow needs a short payback. A brand with patient capital can wait longer and pay more up front.

Payback period is the quiet constraint behind every acquisition decision. Set it before you set budgets.

Pick a target payback you can actually fund. For many DTC brands that is the first order plus 60 to 90 days of repeat revenue.

Put it together

Your CAC ceiling is the contribution margin you earn inside your payback window. If a customer generates 80 dollars of contribution margin within that window, then 80 dollars is your ceiling. Pay less and you grow. Pay more and you are buying revenue at a loss.

  • Calculate contribution margin per order.
  • Add the repeat contribution you reliably earn inside your payback window.
  • That total is the most you can pay to acquire a customer.
  • Compare it to your real, fully loaded CAC, not the platform number.

Why the platform number lies

Ad platforms report the conversions they can claim. They overlap, they double count, and they flatter your best channels. Always check your ceiling against blended CAC: total acquisition spend divided by new customers. That is the number your bank account agrees with.
